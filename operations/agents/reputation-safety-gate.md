# Reputation And Safety Gate

This gate is mandatory before any Agent Council recommendation becomes implementation-ready.

## Public Positioning Guardrails

- Public positioning: Ali is a senior professional/tax leader building practical AI fluency for busy professionals, especially women.
- Private strategy context: lAIdies supports Ali's broader reputation as an inspirational, credible, AI-forward leader.
- Do not publicly mention named live career opportunities or private career discussions.
- Avoid making lAIdies feel like Amazon, Deloitte, tax, or consulting marketing.
- Let success in lAIdies quietly demonstrate leadership: vision, AI fluency, community-building, communication, and execution.

## Mandatory Checklist

Confirm:

- [ ] no confidential workplace information
- [ ] no named live career opportunities
- [ ] no employer-specific implication without approval
- [ ] no unsupported AI/tool/product claims
- [ ] no legal, tax, HR, medical, financial, privacy, or policy guidance presented as official advice
- [ ] no public spotlighting without consent
- [ ] no community answer labeled correct without human review
- [ ] no joke that undermines professional credibility
- [ ] no monetization that makes the brand feel cheap or exploitative
- [ ] no feature that creates unsustainable maintenance burden

## Risk Levels

- Low: routine editorial, design, navigation, or clarity improvement.
- Medium: public claims, user-submitted content, community summaries, or monetization tests.
- High: sensitive workplace topics, named companies/people, high-stakes advice, privacy-impacting features, or public positioning changes.

High-risk items require explicit Ali approval and technical/source review before implementation.

## Community Answer Rule

The chat-room digest can classify answers as helpful, useful, unanswered, repeated, or needs receipts. It must not label an answer as correct without human review.
