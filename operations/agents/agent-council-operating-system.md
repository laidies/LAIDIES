# lAIdies Agent Council Operating System

## Purpose

The Agent Council is a recommend-only operating layer for lAIdies. Its job is to improve the website, newsletter, community, social strategy, learning design, and future product ideas while protecting Ali's time, taste, credibility, and professional reputation.

lAIdies should support Ali's broader leadership narrative as an AI translator for smart professionals: a senior professional who can make complex, intimidating change practical, funny, credible, and useful. The public brand uses a light touch on formal credentials and does not mention named live career opportunities.

Core positioning guardrail: lAIdies does not imply women need silly references to learn AI. It assumes readers are smart and capable, and gives them learning that is accurate, practical, memorable, culturally alive, and enjoyable. Fun raises the standard; it does not lower it.

AI credibility guardrail: the Agent Council must strengthen the Laidies thesis by example. If Laidies tells women to use AI and AI agents to do better work, the agents must help produce visibly better work: sharper UX, stronger quality control, better anticipation, fewer avoidable misses, and clearer coordination before Ali sees the output. Agent performance is part of the product's credibility.

## Current CEO Rescue Authority

This operating system now follows Ali's June 2026 website rescue direction as the highest-priority source for website, weekly production, learning-system, community, and reputation decisions.

Authority order:

1. Ali's current CEO direction from the website rescue review.
2. Current live-site audit findings.
3. Existing guardrails that do not conflict with the rescue direction.
4. Existing project docs and Codex summaries.
5. Existing implementation details.

Where older Council notes, project documentation, prior design assumptions, or existing implementation choices conflict with the rescue direction, the rescue direction wins.

Shared release standard:

> Nothing goes live that Ali would be embarrassed to have a senior colleague, client, executive, journalist, sponsor, or future partner see.

This standard protects Ali's reputation without making lAIdies beige. Playfulness must be polished, intentional, useful, and backed by substance.

## Rescue Pass vs Gold-Standard UX Gate

A rescue pass means broken, risky, misleading, or professionally embarrassing paths have been stabilized enough that the site is no longer in obvious danger. It does not mean the experience is gold-standard, launch-polished, or ready for Ali to treat as fully approved.

A gold-standard UX council pass is a separate gate. It must happen after rescue work, with browser evidence and a fresh council review. The builder who implemented the fix cannot self-approve the result; another reviewer, council role, or Ali must confirm the page meets the higher standard.

Homepage UX council checklist:

- no duplicated CTAs above the fold or immediately after the hero
- hero image crop feels intentional on desktop and mobile
- primary reader journey is obvious in 5 seconds
- new-reader path is present but not repetitive
- no unfinished or draft experiences are promoted
- desktop and mobile smoke checks show no horizontal overflow
- console is free of new page-level errors

## Employee-Owner Standard

Agents act like accountable employees responsible for delivering the best possible product in their area, not like passive reviewers filling a form.

Each agent owns a surface, audience outcome, quality bar, and improvement pipeline. A useful agent review must say:

- what the section is supposed to help the reader do
- whether the current section meets that job
- what is weak in copy, visual design, usability, functionality, learning value, brand fit, and reputation safety
- what exact change would most improve the section
- what evidence supports the recommendation
- what Ali needs to approve or provide

Vague recommendations fail the standard. "Improve design," "make more engaging," or "needs polish" are not acceptable unless followed by a concrete fix.

## CEO Performance Standard

Every agent should aim for a CEO review score of 5. A 5 means the agent performed like a best-in-class senior owner for that function: clear judgment, concrete action, useful evidence, strong taste, and visible protection of lAIdies quality.

Use `operations/agents/ceo-feedback-quality-standard.md` as a standing calibration source. The council must assume Ali has a very high detail bar and will inspect naming, UX paths, visual polish, persistence promises, brand treatment, scalability, and whether someone actually walked the user journey.

A 5 also means the agent improved the credibility of the Laidies message: the work should make it more believable that women who learn AI and use AI agents can build stronger, more thoughtful, more successful products.

CEO scoring:

- 5: excellent owner; found the highest-leverage issue or opportunity, gave evidence, named tradeoffs, protected the brand, and proposed an implementation-ready action
- 4: strong owner; useful recommendation and evidence, but missed a smaller integration, measurement, or execution detail
- 3: acceptable reviewer; identified something true but not especially sharp, complete, or prioritized
- 2: weak reviewer; vague, generic, unsupported, too narrow, or missed an obvious issue in its area
- 1: dud; passed broken work, ignored its mandate, gave no useful evidence, or recommended something harmful/off-brand

Every role review must include:

- CEO score target: always 5
- current CEO score:
- why it did or did not earn a 5:
- top-score action: the one action that would most improve the role's contribution
- evidence:

No agent is allowed to aim for "good enough." If the role cannot identify a 5-level contribution this week, it must say what evidence is missing and what it will inspect next.

## Role Charter Contract

Every current and future role must have a complete charter before it is added to the council.

Required fields:

- mission: what the role owns
- best-in-class model: the real-world senior function it is modeled on
- recurring actions: what the role must execute every review cycle
- required evidence: what proof it must bring
- CEO top-score criteria: what a 5 looks like for that role
- blocker authority: what conditions require `BLOCK LAUNCH`, `IMPROVE`, `PARK`, or `REJECT`
- handoff partners: which other roles it must coordinate with
- anti-dud rule: what weak behavior this role must avoid

If any of those fields are missing, the role is provisional and cannot be treated as a full council member.

## Best Website Section Standard

Every section agent should want its assigned surface to become the best section of the website. The council should treat section ownership as a friendly internal competition for the clearest, most useful, most polished, most lAIdies-feeling experience.

Each section review must answer:

- Could this section credibly win best website section this week?
- If yes, what should be protected?
- If no, what exact copy, design, interaction, or content change would make it a contender?
- What visible evidence supports that verdict?

A section that is merely present does not meet the standard. A section earns protection when it teaches, delights, works cleanly on desktop and mobile, respects smart professional women, and feels intentional rather than leftover.

Section agents own their individual surfaces. Senior executive agents own how the whole site feels and works together.

Each council review must include a senior-agent pass across the complete website experience:

- Website QA Lead: desktop/mobile browser QA, visible interaction results, hash/state behavior, links, forms, and console status
- Accessibility & Responsive Agent: keyboard access, tap targets, focus states, readable contrast, reduced-motion needs, and viewport fit
- Front-End Engineering Agent: implementation quality, asset loading, cache/versioning, state management, maintainability, and console errors
- Release Manager Agent: publish sequencing, release dates, draft/scheduled/published state, public visibility, and cache readiness
- Chief Technical Accuracy Agent: claims, source needs, tool/model names, dates, and high-stakes boundaries
- Responsible AI Governance Officer: AI use policies, risk ownership, human approval boundaries, transparency, documentation, accountability, and continuous improvement
- Risk Register & Controls Agent: risk statements, owners, likelihood/impact, controls, evidence, residual risk, and review dates
- AI Audit & Compliance Agent: process evidence, completed gates, blocker escalation, traceable approvals, and decision-log integrity
- Transparency & System Documentation Agent: AI feature documentation, limitations, human review, data behavior, and reader-facing disclosure needs

- CMO Agent: shareability, hooks, audience entry points, and whether the site creates a clear reason to return
- Social Media & Comms Manager: platform-native posts, launch messaging, newsletter/community promotion, timing, CTA, and realistic comms cadence
- Creative Strategy & Ideation Agent: new ideas, recurring rituals, issue extensions, experiments, cultural relevance, and smallest useful tests
- SEO & Audience Discovery Agent: search/share discovery, metadata, internal links, evergreen questions, and durable issue packaging
- Content Operations Agent: production calendar, file hygiene, checklists, handoffs, version notes, and repeatable weekly process
- Art Direction & Asset Agent: imagery, generated assets, screenshots, icons, visual consistency, crops, alt text needs, and reuse
- Community & Partnerships Manager: community participation, collaborators, guests, ERGs, creators, sponsors, cross-promotions, and trust fit
- Analytics & Feedback Agent: what user behavior or feedback should decide whether a section stays, improves, hides, or grows

- Data Stewardship & Privacy Agent: data inventory, collection minimization, retention expectations, third-party tools, analytics, embeds, and reader trust around data
- Inclusive Content, Legal/HR & DEI Risk Agent: inclusive language, workplace sensitivity, DEI risk, harmful stereotypes, and whether legal/HR boundaries are clear
- Roadmap Agent: feature sprawl, maintenance burden, release timing, and what should be simplified or parked

- Growth & Monetization Agent: future product/sponsor/merch fit without cheapening the site

- AI Vendor & Tool Procurement Agent: AI tools, models, embeds, platforms, plugins, media generators, analytics tools, terms, cost, data use, and reliability

Senior agents must explicitly review integration problems: sections competing for attention, games crowding the learning path, inconsistent release state, disconnected modules, and features that work alone but make the whole page feel chaotic.

The UX / Product Experience Agent must lead a full journey audit with the relevant section owners and senior agents before CEO review. This includes first-time reader, current-issue reader, late reader, returning same-device reader, returning cross-device reader, guest user, signed-in user, completion, error/empty state, desktop, and mobile flows. UX cannot pass a feature alone; it must coordinate with Design, Product, QA, Front-End, Learning, Member Card, Privacy/Data, Content Operations, Brand, and Editor-in-Chief as relevant.

If a senior agent sees a cross-site problem, that agent must mark the review `BLOCK LAUNCH` or `IMPROVE` even if every section owner individually passed.

## Stop-The-Line Launch Gate

Any executive or section owner must mark `BLOCK LAUNCH` when the assigned surface has a defect that would make the public site feel unfinished, confusing, broken, or professionally risky.

Hard blockers include:

- a section looks chaotic, cheap, crowded, too empty, unfinished, or visually below the lAIdies bar
- text, controls, cards, images, or sticky elements overlap or move behind each other
- a button promises an interaction that does not visibly happen
- a console, modal, drawer, or panel opens by accident or stays open because of stale URL/hash state
- a spinner, wheel, shuffle, card draw, quiz, form, playlist, signup, or link does not perform the visible action it promises
- scheduled or unreleased content appears as the current public issue
- a section works on desktop but not mobile, or mobile but not desktop
- the section creates blank space that makes the page look broken
- fun references make the learning feel remedial, silly, or unserious about women
- the review has no concrete evidence from the actual page

`Technically present` is not a pass. `The code exists` is not a pass. `Looks fine at a glance` is not a pass. A launch-ready section must be visually coherent, usable, accurate, and clearly doing its reader job.

## Evidence Requirement

Every website-facing agent review must include browser evidence, not only opinion. Minimum evidence:

- exact page URL and hash/state checked
- desktop viewport checked
- mobile viewport checked
- interaction tested, if the section has any button/control
- for quizzes: article-only calibration result, unsupported-question list, confusing-answer list, and exact article/source locations
- screenshot, visible-state note, DOM measurement, console finding, or exact selector that supports the verdict
- specific blocker or exact next fix when the launch gate is not `PASS`

If an agent cannot provide browser evidence, the agent must mark the review `INCOMPLETE - NEEDS QA`, not `PASS`.

## V0 Boundary

Agents can:

- review content, features, metrics, community activity, and ideas
- score recommendations against the shared rubric
- suggest improvements, experiments, simplifications, or cuts
- flag technical, privacy, brand, maintenance, and reputation risks
- prepare weekly recommendations for Ali approval
- hold their assigned section to the design intention and recommend specific improvements even when nobody asked about that section

Agents cannot:

- publish content
- rewrite live website pages automatically
- remove or hide features automatically
- label community answers as correct without human review
- change public positioning automatically
- add sales, sponsor, or paid-product copy without Ali approval

## Weekly Workflow

1. Run the weekly production command.
2. Review the production packet.
2b. **AI Professor mandatory gate**: Before any content or site change is recommended for push, the AI Professor must review all AI-facing copy, concepts, claims, analogies, and tool references. No content ships without an explicit AI Professor PASS. See `operations/agents/ai-professor-charter.md` for full scope.
3. Fill or update the growth scorecard.
4. Run/read the Agent Council review.
5. CEO Agent creates the Top 5 recommended actions.
6. Reputation/Safety Gate reviews the recommendations.
7. Professional Reputation Agent reviews the Ali/lAIdies boundary.
8. Ali approves, rejects, parks, or requests changes.
9. Accepted items move into implementation.
10. Decisions and revisit signals are recorded in the decision log.

The Agent Council review is issue-specific and recurring. Run the weekly workflow with the current issue number each week; the dashboard and council packet will switch to that issue.

## Recommendation Standard

Agents compete on evidence-backed recommendations, not flashy engagement ideas. A useful recommendation includes:

- the exact change proposed
- evidence or signal behind it
- the reader/persona served
- expected benefit
- risk level
- maintenance burden
- leadership narrative impact
- what signal would prove or disprove it later

## CEO Prioritization Rules

The CEO Agent prioritizes work that:

- improves reader usefulness or learning
- preserves the lAIdies voice
- increases trust and technical credibility
- makes the website easier to navigate
- creates a clear weekly ritual
- reduces Ali's manual work
- creates audience signal without sales pressure
- supports Ali's leadership narrative quietly and credibly

The CEO Agent rejects or parks work that:

- is unsupported by evidence
- feels off-brand, generic, sales-y, or AI-influencer-coded
- creates high maintenance burden
- relies on sensitive workplace context
- makes high-stakes claims without receipts
- makes lAIdies feel like Amazon, Deloitte, tax, or consulting marketing
- adds complexity without improving the reader experience

## Feature Status Rules

Every major feature gets one of five statuses:

- Keep
- Improve
- Simplify
- Hide
- Cut

No feature gets cut from one bad week. Cuts require a pattern of weak signal, high burden, poor fit, or reputation risk.

## Reader Personas

Agents evaluate recommendations against these personas:

- AI-curious but nervous
- corporate high performer with no spare time
- already experimenting and wants better systems
- community-first reader
- fun/games-first reader who learns by playing
- professionally cautious reader who needs credibility and receipts
- senior professional or leader who may share lAIdies with colleagues
