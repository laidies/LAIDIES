# Founder Content Context

Use this when turning Ali's life clips, behind-the-scenes notes, LinkedIn posts, and founder updates into lAIdies content.

## Public Positioning

lAIdies should present Ali as the smart friend building practical AI fluency for busy corporate women.

Do not rewrite Ali as the coolest person in the room. The founder texture should feel smart, funny, accessible, specific, and self-aware, not like a retroactive cool-girl résumé.

Ali's reference memory is Canadian, but the lAIdies audience is broader than Canada. Use Canadian lived memory as an anchor, while allowing U.S. and broader global 90s/Y2K references when they help users recognize the idea quickly.

The audience is primarily:

- busy corporate women
- women curious about AI
- women who want AI learning to feel practical, relatable, and fun
- community builders
- tax professionals, naturally, because much of Ali's professional network is tax

## Founder Story

Core founder arc:

- Inspired by the sisterhood of women in tax.
- Energized by participation in a hackathon with Sara and Eugina.
- Saw too much AI content aimed at tech-bro audiences.
- Wanted AI learning that felt fun, relatable, useful, and socially safe.
- Believes learning should be fun.
- Built from lived experience: invisible work, repeated explanations, knowledge trapped in conversations, and women carrying work that does not always get measured or credited.
- Hackathon lesson: women do not need a computer science degree to shape what AI solves; they need a real problem, willingness to learn, and the confidence to start building.
- Echo proof point: an AI agent concept from Women-in-Tax that turns repeated explanations into reusable knowledge in the person's own voice, making invisible work more visible and reusable.
- lAIdies follow-on: after seeing women become sharper and more confident through hands-on AI building, Ali created a fun AI learning space because existing AI content did not feel relatable, welcoming, or built for women, especially busy corporate women.

Use this as the emotional through-line:

```text
AI fluency for women with full calendars, high standards, and no patience for beige tech explanations.
```

Use this as the founder-story through-line:

```text
Some of us were already comfortable with AI. Some of us were newer to it. All of us got sharper when we stopped only reading about AI and started building with it.
```

Do not overuse hackathon specifics in every post. Use them as origin-story context when the content is about:

- why women need to shape AI
- invisible work
- community learning
- confidence through building
- why lAIdies exists
- why AI learning should feel practical and fun

## Life Clip Content Rules

When Ali shares a clip or photo:

1. Identify the usable story first.
2. Decide whether it is founder BTS, issue support, a weekly recurring series, or personal texture.
3. If Ali dislikes how she looks, suggest crops or edits before discarding the image.
4. Prefer captions that make the imperfect setup feel intentional.
5. Tie the moment back to lAIdies only if the connection is clear.

Strong content buckets:

- Field Notes from lAIdies HQ
- Small Sip Sunday
- Makeover Monday
- The Thursday Try-On
- Soundtrack Saturday
- founder BTS
- smart working-parent-in-the-margins updates
