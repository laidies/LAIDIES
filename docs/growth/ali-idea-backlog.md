## Teaching Analogies

### Terminal / PowerShell / Commands

Commands are like the secret drink menu at Starbucks.

The apps and buttons you see are the printed menu. But there is a whole other set of options that are not listed on the menu. If you know what to order, and say it exactly right, you can get exactly what you want.

Use this for:

- Terminal
- PowerShell
- command
- script
- local server
- "why did the command fail?"

## Reference Closet Commerce / Watch Links

Idea: make the Reference Closet more useful by linking referenced objects, movies, TV shows, books, beauty items, and nostalgic accessories to places readers can buy, watch, or revisit them.

Possible labels:

- Shop the Closet
- Watch the Closet
- Open the Receipt
- Where to Find It
- Add to Cart, But Make It Canon

Use cases:

- Product/object references: link to Amazon or another retailer where readers can buy modern equivalents.
- Movies/TV references: link to streaming, rental, or official watch pages.
- Books/music references: link to buy/listen/watch where appropriate.
- Newsletter/social tie-in: every issue can include one "from the closet" item with why it matters to the AI lesson.

Guardrails:

- Clearly disclose affiliate links if affiliate links are used.
- Do not imply endorsement by Amazon, streaming services, studios, brands, or creators.
- Do not foreground Ali's employer identity; this is a lAIdies resource/shopping feature, not an Amazon-branded feature.
- Verify current product availability, prices, and streaming availability before publishing because these change.
- Prefer "inspired by" items for nostalgic fashion/beauty/object references instead of exact logo-heavy brand copying unless linking to the real official product page.
- Keep the lesson first. The shop/watch link should support the analogy, not turn the Reference Closet into a random storefront.

Possible card fields:

- why it belongs in lAIdies

- availability checked date

## Social

- Reel idea: use the funny head-move structure from the White Chicks / Michelle Branch meme format.
- Guardrail: use platform-available/licensed audio and do not rely on uploading copyrighted clips outside platform rules.
- Meme/social hook: "I've got one hand in my pocket, and the other one is prompting AI."
  - Reference: Alanis Morissette, "Hand in My Pocket"
  - Best use: caption, Reel cover, quote card, or newsletter aside about practical AI use becoming part of daily work.
- Recurring reaction format: Terry Crews / White Chicks "A Thousand Miles" clip.
  - Clip idea: show a lAIdies website/newsletter statement first, then use the Terry Crews reaction as if he is reading the site and is immediately emotionally in.
  - Why it fits: "A Thousand Miles" by Vanessa Carlton is already in the lAIdies playlist, so the reference can connect social, website, and House DJ energy.
  - Repeatable framing: "When the lAIdies site says..." / "When the newsletter understands your exact workday..." / "When the prompt finally gives useful output..."
  - Guardrail: use native platform audio/remix/green-screen features where available; do not upload ripped copyrighted video or audio outside platform rules.
- Weekly recurring series idea: The Thursday Try-On.
  - Purpose: Thursday follows the Wednesday issue drop and turns the lesson into the "homework" readers were supposed to practice.
  - Structure: "Yesterday we learned [concept]. Today, try it on."
  - Best use: Story sequence, carousel, or short Reel that sends readers to the Try-On Debrief.
  - CTA: "Bring the messy version to the Try-On Debrief."
  - Example Issue 1: "Yesterday we opened the tab. Today, give ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini the same low-risk prompt."
  - Example Issue 2: "Yesterday we learned specificity. Today, ask AI once vaguely, then ask again with audience, context, tone, length, and what not to include."
