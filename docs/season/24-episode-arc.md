# lAIdies Season 1 Bible

## Series Logline

A smart, busy woman stops feeling behind, learns to delegate to machines, keeps her standards, builds her squad, and becomes the person other women come to when the future gets confusing.

## Season Shape

Season 1 is 24 weekly Wednesday episodes in four acts. The reader journey is:

Outsider -> beginner -> practitioner -> strategist -> leader -> mentor.

Every issue should move both the AI fluency and the identity story forward. The issue is not only "about a topic." It is an episode in the story of a woman becoming more confident, useful, discerning, and generous with AI.

## Act 4 AI Squad Payoff

These named personas should be seeded before Act 4, then built deliberately in the final act:

- Miranda: ruthless editor/proofreader, inspired by The Devil Wears Prada.
- Carrie: writer and communicator, inspired by Sex and the City.
- Sophia: wise advisor and institutional memory, inspired by The Golden Girls.
- Elle: researcher who finds what everyone else missed, inspired by Legally Blonde.
- Cher: organizer and matcher, inspired by Clueless.
- Buffy: slayer of scary, overwhelming, avoided tasks, inspired by Buffy.

## Foreshadowing Rules

- Seed the AI employee squad concept early: every skill eventually becomes part of the reader's AI team.
- Seed ethics and bias early. Some hesitation is not technophobia; some of it is discernment.
- Seed the taste and judgment theme throughout. AI can give options; the reader still decides what good looks like.
- Frame AI interaction as management practice from Issue 2 onward.
- Let every issue include one thing the reader could use at work within 24 hours.

## Episode Module Pack

Every issue should produce one compact module pack:

- Hero image
- Quiz
- Card pack
- Try-On prompt
- Community prompt or live-room thread
- Glossary/reference update
- Website-only Hot Goss items, when relevant
- Newsletter links to the relevant website modules

The article teaches. The website carries the interactive activity, fast-moving news, and archive value.

## TLDR Rule

The TLDR is a single flowing episode description, not bullet points. It should read like:

Episode X: The one in which [hook], discovers [insight], compares [AI concept] to [pop culture analogy], and [homework teaser].

When all 24 TLDRs are stitched together, they should tell the full transformation story from outsider to mentor.

## Technical And Editorial Guardrails

- AI fluency is executive literacy, not "keeping up with tech bros."
- Fun carries real substance. Do not reduce the fun, but never let it become fluff.
- Distinguish companies, products, and models.
- Do not say AI lies unless clearly using shorthand. Incorrect generation is not deception.
- Use current model names and product details, and verify them before publication.
- Analogies should clarify the concept, not replace the concept.
- The biggest risk is being too cute without rigor. The audience has high standards.
