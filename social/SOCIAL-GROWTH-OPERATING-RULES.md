# Social Growth Operating Rules

These rules apply to every weekly lAIdies social batch.

## North Star

Social content is not filler. It exists to maximize visibility, readership, saves, shares, website clicks, and community participation while still sounding exactly like lAIdies.

Every weekly batch must help a busy corporate woman think:

- "This is for me."
- "I want to send this to someone."
- "I should save this."
- "I want to read the full issue."
- "I want to click the game/quiz/card/thread on the website."

## Website Traffic Rule

The website is the destination. Social is the invitation.

Every weekly social package must include a specific website traffic path:

- 1 post or Story points to the full issue.
- 1 post or Story points to a fun feature: quiz, card pack, Ask LAIDY, Madame CLAI-O, Dream Phone, Girl Talk, Glossary Rolodex, Hot Goss, House DJ, Reference Closet, Try-On Debrief, Burn Book, or Member Cards.
- 1 caption or Story frame gives people a concrete reason to click now: take the quiz, pull a card, try the prompt, ask LAIDY, get the glossary term, or read the full example.

Use `social/WEBSITE-LINK-MAP.md` to choose the exact destination URL or anchor before making the Story, caption, LinkedIn post, or Canva notes.

Avoid vague CTAs like "check out the website." Use specific CTAs:

- "Take the Issue 02 quiz on the site."
- "Pull this week's trading card."
- "Ask LAIDY the question you do not want to ask at work."
- "Read the full issue before your next vague ChatGPT spiral."
- "Try the prompt on the site, then bring your messy version back to the Room."

## Source Of Truth

Do not create social content from the thin generated Instagram kit alone.

Use this source order:

1. `content/issues/issue-XX.md`
2. `email/buttondown/issue-XX.md`
3. `issues/issue-XX.html`
4. `content/episodes/issue-XX.json`
5. `content/site/quizzes.json`
6. `content/site/card-packs.json`
7. Homepage module copy in `index.html`
8. `REFERENCE-CLOSET.md`
9. `INSTAGRAM-CONTENT-BANK.md`
10. Generated `social/episodes/issue-XX-instagram-kit.md`

The generated kit is a starting checklist, not the final creative.

## Weekly Content Mix

Each issue needs a social package with:

- 1 discovery Reel: hook from the issue, fast payoff, clear "read the issue" CTA.
- 1 saveable carousel: one useful concept, not the whole issue.
- 1 website feature post: quiz, card pack, Ask LAIDY, Madame CLAI-O, Dream Phone, Girl Talk, Glossary Rolodex, Hot Goss, House DJ, or Reference Closet, with a direct click CTA.
- 1 Story sequence: poll/question/link sticker that points to the latest issue or a specific website feature.
- 1 community prompt: Ask the Room, Try-On Debrief, Burn Book, Wins, or Member Cards.

If the week is chaotic, minimum viable package:

- 1 Reel
- 1 carousel
- 1 Story sequence with link
- 1 community question

## Sustainable Volume Rule

Do not plan 14 social pieces per day as the default. That is too much for lAIdies unless most of the pieces are lightweight Story frames around a launch, event, or community moment.

Default weekly target:

- 3-5 feed posts per week total: Reels, carousels, or static posts.
- 1 lightweight Story presence every day so the account keeps the pink ring and looks active.
- 3-7 Story frames on active Story days: issue drop, website feature push, poll, question, or community moment.
- 1-2 website click moments per week minimum.
- 1 community prompt per week.

Daily Story minimum:

- 1 frame is enough on quiet days.
- It can be a repost, poll, question, quote from the issue, link reminder, card pull, quiz prompt, or quick "AI question of the day."
- Do not treat the daily Story as a full new idea. It is the always-on signal.

Daily Story prompt menu:

- "What does Madame CLAI-O tell you today?" Link to Madame CLAI-O or the week's vibe/check-in feature.
- "Big meeting? Ask your fAIry godmother for guidance." Link to Ask LAIDY, a prompt deck, or the week's workplace script.
- "Call the Dream Phone." Link to Dream Phone or a playful decision/helper feature.
- "Pull today's lAIdies card." Link to the weekly card pack.
- "Take the 60-second quiz." Link to the current issue quiz.
- "What AI question are you avoiding today?" Use a question sticker, then send people to Ask LAIDY or the Room.
- "Messy prompt hotline is open." Invite replies and point to the Try-On or prompt deck.

Launch week or special push:

- Up to 1-2 feed posts per day for 2-3 days.
- Up to 7-10 Story frames on the main launch day.
- Then return to the default rhythm.

Quality bar:

- A post should exist because it has a clear hook, useful payoff, or website click reason.
- Do not split weak ideas into more posts just to hit volume.
- Reuse one strong weekly idea across formats instead of inventing 14 new ideas per day.

## Optimization Rule

Every post must be designed around one primary signal.

- Reels: reach, watch time, follows, profile visits.
- Carousels: saves, sends, dwell time, comments.
- Stories: replies, poll votes, link clicks.
- Feature posts: website clicks and repeat visits.
- Community prompts: replies, comments, and future content mining.

Do not post generic "new issue is live" content unless it has a strong hook or a specific website action.

## Copy Rules

Use exact lAIdies language from the issue and website whenever possible.

Good source lines:

- "AI fluency for women with full calendars, high standards, and no patience for beige tech explanations."
- "No one needs to tour the whole mall before buying the lip gloss."
- "ICQ walked so these rooms could run."
- "Small sips, big moves."
- "The 90s/Y2K break room for small sips, useful chaos, and better questions."
- "The one in which she realizes AI is already being added to the invisible load, says 'ugh, as if,' and opens the tab anyway."
- "Prompting is not coding. It is delegation."
- "The Spice Girls did not say 'tell me what you vaguely want.'"
- "Be specific. Be bold. Be David Rose about it."

Avoid:

- generic AI tips
- generic empowerment copy
- Canva-generated marketing copy
- workout language like "rep" when "small sip" would fit better
- vague "check out the website" CTAs
- carousels that summarize everything without giving one saveable idea

## Website Feature Rule

Every issue should send people to at least one fun website feature.

Strong pairings:

- Issue 01: Ask LAIDY, Five-Minute Try-On, Issue 01 Quiz, Trading Card Pack, Ask the Room.
- Issue 02: Trading Card Pack, Girl Talk Prompt Deck, Madame CLAI-O, Dream Phone, Try-On Debrief, Glossary Rolodex.

Use `Madame CLAI-O`, not `Madame Cleo`.

## Visual Rule

Do not let Canva invent the brand.

Visuals should be based on:

- real logo assets
- real issue images
- dark mocha header/doodle texture
- warm blush paper
- hot pink neon accent
- mocha text
- teal/gold support accents
- editorial magazine layouts
- trading cards, Rolodex, phone, closet, card pack, quiz, mix CD, receipts, and Caboodle metaphors

Canva is for assembly and editing. The lAIdies website is the visual reference.

## Current Platform Notes

Checked June 2026:

- Meta's Instagram Best Practices hub covers creation, engagement, reach, monetization, and guidelines, and is designed to help creators improve content and growth.
- Meta says Instagram provides creator guidance on post frequency, attention capture, Reel length, trending audio, hashtags, engagement metrics, and reach.
- Meta has also added stronger creation and insights tools for Reels, carousels, Stories, drafts, filters, replays, and retention charts.
- Current third-party platform analysis continues to treat Reels as useful for discovery and carousels as strong for save/share-heavy education.

Practical implication for lAIdies:

- Use Reels to make new people recognize the problem.
- Use carousels to make them save and send the solution.
- Use Stories to make them answer.
- Use website features to make them click and stay.
- Use community prompts to turn readers into the room.
