# lAIdies Instagram Strategy

This is the low-lift Instagram plan for `@we.are.laidies`: grow awareness, make AI feel approachable, drive readers to the website/newsletter, and turn followers into a real community.

The strategy is built for a side project. It assumes Ali has a full-time job and needs a repeatable rhythm, not a second full-time job.

Use this with:

- `social/episodes/issue-XX-instagram-kit.md` for episode-specific launch content.
- `INSTAGRAM-CONTENT-BANK.md` for evergreen backup posts, hooks, and prompts.
- `social/SOCIAL-GROWTH-OPERATING-RULES.md` for the weekly visibility/readership rules.

Important: episode kits are a production helper, not the final creative source. For each weekly batch, pull from the full issue Markdown, Buttondown draft, issue page, homepage modules, quizzes, card packs, community prompts, and website feature copy before finalizing social content.

Episode kits are generated from `content/episodes/issue-XX.json`:

```powershell
node scripts/build-episode-assets.js
```

## The Simple Goal

Make busy professional women think:

1. "This is for me."
2. "This made AI less intimidating."
3. "I want to send this to a friend."
4. "I should follow before I forget."
5. "I might actually try the weekly prompt."

## Visibility And Readership Rule

Every weekly social package should be designed to create one or more of these outcomes:

- reach new people
- earn saves
- earn sends/shares
- drive website clicks
- drive full-issue reads
- produce replies/comments that can become future content

Website traffic is not optional. Each weekly package needs at least one post or Story that sends people to the latest issue and at least one post or Story that sends people to a specific interactive feature on the website.

The standard weekly package is:

- 1 discovery Reel
- 1 saveable carousel
- 1 website feature post
- 1 Story sequence
- 1 community prompt

Website features are part of the social strategy, not bonus extras: Ask LAIDY, Madame CLAI-O, Dream Phone, Girl Talk, quizzes, card packs, Glossary Rolodex, Hot Goss, House DJ, Reference Closet, Try-On Debrief, Burn Book, and Member Cards.

## Website Click Loop

Use the same loop every week:

1. Instagram makes the problem recognizable.
2. The caption or Story gives one specific reason to click.
3. The website gives the deeper payoff: full issue, quiz, card, game, prompt, glossary, or community feature.
4. The next Story/community prompt brings people back to share what happened.

Strong website CTAs:

- "Take the quiz on the site."
- "Pull this week's card."
- "Read the full issue."
- "Try the prompt on the site."
- "Ask LAIDY before you ask your boss."
- "Go to Madame CLAI-O for the week's AI vibe check."

Weak website CTAs:

- "Check out the website."
- "Link in bio."
- "Learn more."
- "New post is up."

## What Instagram Is For

Instagram is not the full classroom. The website/newsletter is the classroom.

Instagram is the hallway conversation:

- quick confidence boosts
- funny AI analogies
- short prompt demos
- relatable workplace moments
- community questions
- reminders to read the Wednesday episode

## Source-Informed Platform Assumptions

Instagram changes constantly, so these are working assumptions, not guarantees.

- Reels are the main discovery format. Use them to reach people who do not follow yet.
- Carousels are for saves, shares, and "I need this later" value.
- Stories are for relationship, replies, polls, and lightweight community rituals.
- Original content matters. Avoid reposted/watermarked TikToks.
- Watch time, sends/shares, saves, replies, and repeat engagement matter more than vanity posting volume.
- Short, specific, emotionally clear hooks usually beat vague educational intros.

Useful reference points:

- Meta launched an Instagram "Best Practices" education hub to help creators improve creation, engagement, reach, and growth.
- Meta/Sprout have described Reels as a major Instagram attention surface.
- Instagram ranking is not one single algorithm. Feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore behave differently.

## Weekly Cadence

Minimum viable cadence:

- 3 Reels per week
- 2 carousel posts per week
- 1 lightweight Story presence daily
- 1 community prompt per week

The feed-post number does not include Stories. Stories last 24 hours and also keep the account visibly active with the pink ring, so lAIdies should have at least one lightweight Story frame every day. Do not use 14 pieces per day as the normal cadence. That is a campaign-day maximum only if most of the pieces are Story frames and the week has a real launch reason. For a normal issue week, aim for quality and repetition across formats, not constant posting.

Daily Story prompts can be tiny and branded:

- "What does Madame CLAI-O tell you today?"
- "Big meeting? Ask your fAIry godmother for guidance."
- "Call the Dream Phone."
- "Pull today's lAIdies card."
- "Take the 60-second quiz."
- "What AI question are you avoiding today?"
- "Messy prompt hotline is open."

Best posting rhythm:

- Monday: Reel, "AI confidence / workplace moment"
- Tuesday: Carousel, "saveable prompt or checklist"
- Wednesday: Episode drop Reel + Stories
- Thursday: Story poll + community prompt
- Friday: Carousel or Reel, "weekly win / AI try-on"
- Weekend: light Story only, unless there is a launch or community moment

If the week is chaotic:

- Post 1 Reel.
- Post 1 carousel.
- Re-share both to Stories.
- Ask 1 poll.

That still counts.

## Content Pillars

### 1. AI Without the Tech Bro Fog

Purpose: make AI terms feel plain-English and culturally sticky.

Examples:

- "AI is not Google in a blazer."
- "A prompt is a brief, not a wish."
- "Hallucination is Regina George confidence with no receipts."

Best formats:

- Reels
- carousel explainers
- Story quiz

### 2. Prompt Glow-Ups

Purpose: show the difference between vague asks and useful asks.

Structure:

- Bad prompt
- Why it failed
- Better prompt
- Copy/paste version

Best formats:

- carousel
- Reel with screen recording or talking head
- Story "which prompt wins?"

### 3. Workplace Scripts

Purpose: give professional women language they can use today.

Examples:

- ask for more time
- push back without sounding chaotic
- summarize a meeting
- prepare for a stakeholder conversation
- turn messy notes into a concise update

Best formats:

- Reel
- carousel
- story Q&A

### 4. The Try-On

Purpose: make learning feel like a 10-minute challenge.

Examples:

- "Ask AI to rewrite one email three ways."
- "Paste a meeting agenda and ask what decision is being avoided."
- "Ask AI what your VP will ask next."

Best formats:

- Wednesday/Thursday Stories
- Friday recap
- carousel

### 5. The lAIdies Room

Purpose: move from audience to community.

Examples:

- "What AI question are you embarrassed to ask at work?"
- "What tool are you trying?"
- "What would you teach someone else?"
- "Where are you in your AI journey?"

Best formats:

- Story poll
- question sticker
- carousel CTA
- community page link

### 6. 90s/Y2K Analogy Hooks

Purpose: make the content shareable and branded.

Examples:

- Cher's closet computer
- Spice Girls specificity
- Dolly 9 to 5 energy
- Buffy courage
- Elle evidence
- David specificity

Best formats:

- Reels
- memes/static posts
- carousel cover hooks

## The Weekly 90-Minute Creation Workflow

Do this once per week.

### 15 minutes: Pick the week theme

Choose one:

- prompting
- hallucinations
- tool choice
- meeting prep
- privacy
- email writing
- confidence
- community question

### 20 minutes: Draft 5 hooks

Use the generated episode kit first, then pull backup ideas from the content bank. Do not start from a blank page.

### 25 minutes: Create 2 carousels

Use one Canva template:

- cover
- 4 value slides
- CTA slide

### 20 minutes: Batch record 3 Reels

Use the same setup:

- phone vertical
- good window light
- quiet background
- 15-35 seconds each
- first line is the hook

### 10 minutes: Schedule or save drafts

If you cannot schedule, save drafts in Instagram and set calendar reminders.

## Easy Production Setup

Use this simple setup every time:

- phone camera in vertical mode
- face near a window
- plain background or laptop/desk
- captions on every Reel
- 9:16 format for Reels and Stories
- carousel square or 4:5 vertical
- hot pink, mocha, blush, teal, gold brand colors
- use the same cover style every week

## Posting Checklist

Before posting any Reel:

- Hook appears in the first 1-2 seconds.
- On-screen text says the main idea.
- Caption includes a clear takeaway.
- CTA asks for one action: save, send, comment, or try.
- No TikTok watermark.
- Cover is readable in the grid.

Before posting any carousel:

- Cover makes a busy woman stop.
- Slide 2 pays off the cover immediately.
- Each slide has one point.
- Last slide tells people exactly what to do.
- Caption includes a copy/paste prompt if relevant.

Before posting Stories:

- Use at least one interaction sticker: poll, question, quiz, or slider.
- Keep each story frame to one idea.
- End with a link or reply prompt.

## Daily Engagement Routine

This is the part that helps the account feel alive.

10 minutes after posting:

- Reply to comments.
- Reply to DMs.
- Like thoughtful replies.
- Pin the best comment if useful.

10 minutes later that day:

- Comment on 5 relevant posts from women in tech, AI, leadership, marketing, HR, operations, product, and workplace creators.
- Share 1 relevant post to Stories with a lAIdies take.

Do not spam. Be useful, specific, and human.

## Link Strategy

Instagram does not make links easy, so keep the CTA simple.

Bio link should point to:

- homepage
- latest episode
- subscribe
- community/profile intake

Story links should point to:

- latest issue
- specific quiz
- specific card pack
- specific feature or game
- member profile form
- specific community thread

Caption CTA options:

- "Comment PROMPT and I will send the copy/paste version."
- "Save this for your next messy email."
- "Send this to the friend who keeps saying she needs to learn AI."
- "Read the full episode at the link in bio."
- "Tell us where you are in your AI journey."

## Metrics To Track

Track weekly, not obsessively every hour.

Reels:

- views
- average watch time if available
- shares/sends
- follows from Reel
- comments

Carousels:

- saves
- shares
- profile visits
- follows

Stories:

- replies
- poll votes
- link clicks
- completion

Community:

- profile forms started/submitted
- comments on room threads
- repeated questions
- user-generated wins/prompts

## What To Do With Results

Every Friday, ask:

1. What got shared?
2. What got saved?
3. What got replies?
4. What felt easy to make?
5. What should become a series?

Repeat winners. Do not reinvent the brand every week.

## The First 30 Days

Primary goal: prove which hooks work.

Do not over-optimize:

- test Reels
- test carousels
- ask questions in Stories
- invite people into the Room
- collect member profiles

Target output:

- 12 Reels
- 8 carousels
- 12 Story sequences
- 4 community prompts

- make AI less intimidating
