# lAIdies Website Brief

## What Is lAIdies?

**Series name:** lAIdies (lowercase l, uppercase AI, lowercase dies)
**Tagline:** Girl Power Meets Machine Power
**Launch URL:** wearelaidies.com

A 24-episode weekly Wednesday newsletter series teaching AI fluency to women in corporate roles. Think: your smartest girlfriend explaining AI over drinks, packed with 90s/2000s pop culture references, practical tips, and the kind of energy that makes you actually want to keep reading.

---

## Brand Identity

**Audience:** Women 30-50, senior professionals, incredibly busy, high standards. They grew up on Sex and the City, Mean Girls, Clueless, Legally Blonde, Buffy, Spice Girls, Dolly Parton, Golden Girls, Devil Wears Prada, Schitt's Creek, Britney Spears.

**Tone:** Conversational, funny, warm, empowering. Never salesy, never preachy, never corporate. Like a group chat, not a lecture.

**Visual Brand:**
- Primary accent: Hot pink neon (#ff2d9b)
- Dark background for header/footer: Deep mocha-brown (#100805)
- Body background: Warm blush (#fdf8fb)
- Body text: Mocha-brown (#100805) — not black
- Fonts: Inter (body), Playfair Display (headings), Dancing Script (brand name/logo references), JetBrains Mono (code/badges)
- Header/footer texture: Hot pink 90s doodle pattern (hearts, butterfly clips, flip phones, martini glasses, lipstick, peace signs) on dark background using lighten blend mode
- Logo: Neon pink cursive "lAIdies" with bold AI and a circuit-board heart, tagline "GIRL POWER MEETS MACHINE POWER"

**Cultural DNA:** 90s/2000s girl power meets modern professional life. References span: pop culture (films, TV, music), fashion (low-rise denim, butterfly clips, Wet n Wild), tech nostalgia (MSN Messenger, Razr phones, mix CDs), and lifestyle (Claire's, Bath & Body Works, Blockbuster).

## The Season Arc (24 Episodes)

### Series Logline
A smart, busy woman stops feeling behind, learns to delegate to machines, keeps her standards, builds her squad, and becomes the person other women come to when the future gets confusing.

### Structure: 4 Acts

**Act 1: "The Awakening" (Issues 1-6)** — Losing the fear, getting comfortable

- Identity shift: From outsider to beginner.

## Website Requirements

### Pages Needed

**1. Home / Landing**
- Hero: Logo + tagline + "24 episodes. New one every Wednesday."
- Brief explainer of what lAIdies is
- Latest issue featured prominently
- "Start from Episode 1" CTA
- Instagram + subscribe hooks

**2. All Episodes (Archive)**
- Grid or list of all published episodes
- Each shows: number, title, one-line episode description, thumbnail image
- Click to read full article
- Clear Act/chapter grouping (Act 1, Act 2, etc.)
- Locked/coming soon for unpublished episodes (builds anticipation)

**3. Individual Episode Pages**
- Full article content (same as the HTML newsletters)
- Previous/Next navigation
- "Previously on lAIdies" recap
- Images, stat blocks, glossary sections all styled consistently

**4. The Squad (eventually — Act 4)**
- Character profiles for each AI employee
- How to build them
- Templates/resources for each

**5. Glossary / Who's Who**
- Living dictionary that builds over time
- Every term defined across all issues lives here
- Every company/person/tool mentioned gets a one-paragraph explainer
- Searchable
- Written in lAIdies voice (with references)

**6. About**
- Who's behind lAIdies (Ali + Savina)
- The story of how it started (hackathon → newsletter)
- Link to LinkedIn profiles

**7. Community / Subscribe**
- Email subscribe (for notifications)
- Instagram link + QR code
- Future: reader submissions for the lAIdies Challenge

### Design Notes

- The website should feel like an editorial magazine, NOT a blog
- The doodle pattern (90s icons in hot pink on dark background) should appear in header/footer as texture
- Images are editorial, packed with Easter eggs referencing pop culture
- The butterfly clip rating system (0-10 clips) is the recurring visual element
- Pull quotes and stat blocks should match the newsletter styling
- Mobile-first — most readers will find this via LinkedIn/Instagram on their phones
- Dark mocha header/footer with neon logo, warm blush body, pink accents throughout
- Branded section headers with fun subtitles explaining purpose

### Engagement Features

- Easter egg counter ("How many references did you spot?")
- Lipstick rating interactive element (for homework)
- Episode progress tracker ("You're on Episode X of 24")
- Share buttons optimized for LinkedIn
- "Send to a friend" with pre-written message
- Comments section for lAIdies Challenge responses
